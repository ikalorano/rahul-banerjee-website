Voluntary Social Work

More than my research work I have given greater importance to my work as a voluntary social activist among the Bhil Adivasi indigenous people in western Madhya Pradesh State in India. The organisation I helped in setting up for carrying out development work in western Madhya Pradesh, Dhas Gramin Vikas Kendra, has been awarded the prestigious Times of India – J P Morgan Social Impact Award 2011 in the category of advocacy and empowerment.
My alma mater Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur has honoured me with the Distinguished Alumnus Award in 2019. I have now set up another organisation Mahila Jagat Lihaaz Samiti to pursue gender equity and ecological sustainability.
The details of this work are as follows –

  • 2016 - : Resumed reproductive health and rights work with poor Adivasi and Dalit women in slums in cities and in villages and work on survival edge technology consisting of sustainable agriculture, natural resource conservation and renewable energy through the organisation Mahila Jagat Lihaaz Samiti.
  • 2011- : Founded in Delhi, along with other reputed social workers, the organisation, Development and Justice Initiative, for nationwide work for the benefit of the underprivileged sections of society and have been contributing to its activities and governance.
  • 2008 - : Began systematic work that is continuing on ameliorating the adverse conditions under which the migrant tribal workers from Madhya Pradesh work in Gujarat as a new programme of action of the Khedut Mazdoor Chetna Sangath in association with the organisation Centre for Labour Research and Action of which I am a founding member.
  • 2001 - : Began work that is continuing with the urban slum population of Indore city for their right to housing and basic civic amenities in collaboration with the NGO Deenbandhu Samajik Sansthan. Contributed in the formulation of an alternative urban plan that addressed the needs of the poor and deprived sections of the city.
  • 2000 - : Began work on the rejuvenation of traditional Bhil adivasi indigenous culture which has at present evolved into the mass organisation Adivasi Riti Badhao Tola.
  • 1996 - : Began work on Bhil adivasi women’s reproductive health and rights which has evolved at present into the organisation Kansari Nu Vadavno.
